Transportation systems play a vital role in the growth and development of economies. Reliable, cost effective and sustainable modes help in rapid movement of people and goods, not only within a country but also between countries. Considering the ubiquitous nature of the transportation system and the crucial role played by it in the lives of people, it is imperative that the problems associated with the transport systems are addressed in a time bound manner by the scientific community. It goes without saying that the problems associated with the transportation system get accentuated in urban areas.

Growing urbanization, rising vehicle ownership, globalization of economies and shortage of resources has posed major challenges to the transportation sector. Mobility, accessibility and safety continue to be daunting challenges to transportation professionals’ world over. In this context, cutting edge research that could provide sustainable solutions to transport related problems is the need of the hour.

The 10th Conference on Transportation Systems Engineering and Management (CTSEM 2024) aims to bring together researchers, academicians and engineers in different areas of Transportation Engineering and Management. The objectives of this 10th CTSEM are to provide an opportunity for students, research scholars, academicians, scientists and transportation professionals to present their work exchange ideas, network with the peers; learn about advancements and applications in their fields; minimize the duplication of research efforts and thus arrive at future directions for research.

Guidelines for Full Length Paper

  1. Full paper submissions are invited on areas covered in the described themes.

  2. The word template for paper submission can be found here.

  3. Authors can edit the above template and submit their full paper on the paper submission portal of CTSEM 2024.

  4. The full paper length in the prescribed format should not be more than 10 pages.

  5. There are no restrictions on the number of words, tables, and figures. However, the figures should be clearly visible (at least 300 dpi).

  6. Authors are requested to adhere to the given format for font size, line spacing, indentation, and table design.

  7. Please note that selected and peer-reviewed keynote and contributed presentations of the conference will be published by Springer. Publication is contingent upon the final quality and ethics checks by Springer. Only papers that meet the Springer requirements will be published.

About VNIT

The 10th Conference on Transportation Systems Engineering and Management (CTSEM 2024) is being organized by VNIT Nagpur during 19th - 21st July 2024 in the Department of Civil Engineering.

Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology, Nagpur is one of the thirty National Institutes of Technology in the country. Earlier, the Institute was known as Visvesvaraya Regional College of Engineering (VRCE). It was established in the year 1960 under the scheme sponsored by Govt. of India and Govt. of Maharashtra. The college was started in June 1960 by amalgamating the State Govt. Engineering College functioning at Nagpur since July 1956. In the meeting held in October 1962, the Governing Board of the College resolved to name it after the eminent engineer, planner, and statesman of the country Bharat Ratna Sir M. Visvesvaraya. The Central Government declared VNIT Nagpur as an Institute of National Importance along with all other NITs under the National Institutes of Technology Act, 2007. The Act was brought into force from 15th August 2007.

The Department of Civil Engineering is one of the finest and oldest engineering departments of the Institute and stands with an immortal reputation. The department was formed along with the Institute itself and therefore is as old. The Department has experienced faculty members who endeavour to produce the finest engineers. The alumni of the department are widespread in India and abroad, occupying high positions in their respective fields. The department has an intake of 120 students per year under UG Course and 100 per year under PG Courses. The department aims to contribute effectively to the National Endeavour of producing a quality human resource of world-class standard in civil engineering by developing a sustainable technical education system to meet the changing technological needs of the Country incorporating relevance of social concerns and to build an environment to create and propagate innovative technologies for the economic development of the nation.
